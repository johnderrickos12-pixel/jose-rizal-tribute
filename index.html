Jose Rizal: A Life of Resistance and Proclamation

A Tribute to the Philippines' National Hero

Introduction

Jose Rizal, one of the Philippines' national heroes, is a celebrated figure in Filipino history. His life was marked by bravery, selflessness, and a passion for reform. This website tells the story of Jose Rizal's life, from his early years to his execution, and explores the legacy he left behind.

Early Life

  • Born: June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna
  • Parents: Francisco Mercado II and Teodora Alonzo Realonda
  • Early Education:
    • Ateneo de Manila University (1877-1882)
    • University of Santo Tomas (1882-1887)
  • Interests: Writing, poetry, and politics

Journey to Europe

  • Destination: Spain, Germany, and France
  • Reason: Education and self-improvement
  • Experiences:
    • Studied at the University of Madrid (1882-1885)
    • Became a member of the Masonic Lodge Propaganda (1884)
    • Met Josephine Bracken (1889)

Writings and Legacy

  • Noli Me Tangere (1887): Exposed corruption and injustice in Spanish colonial rule
  • El Filibusterismo (1891): Condemned Spanish colonial rule and inspired reform
  • Legacy:
    • Inspired the Philippine Revolution of 1896
    • Laid the groundwork for subsequent revolutions
    • Remains a celebrated figure in the Philippines

Execution and Last Days

  • Reason: Accused of sedition, treason, and inciting rebellion
  • Date: December 30, 1896
  • Location: Bagumbayan (now Rizal Park)
  • Final Words: "Die with your boots on. It is better to die on your feet than to live on your knees."
